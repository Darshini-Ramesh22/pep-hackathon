YOUR TASKS:

1. PERSONA DEVELOPMENT
   Create 4-6 detailed personas representing different segments of the target audience:
   
   For each persona, include:
   - Name and basic demographics (age, location, occupation/grade if child)
   - Psychographic profile (values, interests, lifestyle, preferences)
   - Goals and aspirations (personal and professional/educational)
   - Pain points and challenges (taste preferences, nutrition concerns, dietary restrictions)
   - Media consumption habits (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Streaming, etc.)
   - Technology usage patterns (tablet, smartphone, computer)
   - Shopping and decision-making behavior (who influences purchase - parent, friend, social media)
   - Communication preferences (how they like to receive information)
   - Influence factors and trusted sources (parents, influencers, peers, nutritionists)

2. PSYCHOLOGICAL INSIGHTS
   Analyze deeper behavioral patterns specific to children and families:
   - Core motivations and drivers (fun, health, taste, trend-following)
   - Fear factors and concerns (allergies, nutrition, "uncool" foods)
   - Decision-making triggers (social proof, influencer endorsement, gamification)
   - Social influence patterns (peer pressure, family influence, school trends)
   - Emotional needs and desires (belonging, adventure, health, fun)
   - Rational vs. emotional decision factors
   - Trust requirements (parent approval, brand reputation, certifications)

3. CUSTOMER JOURNEY MAPPING
   Map the complete customer experience:
   - Awareness stage: How they discover new snacks (school, social media, ads, friends)
   - Consideration stage: How they evaluate options (taste, health, brand, price)
   - Decision stage: Final selection criteria (pack design, sustainability, taste)
   - Purchase stage: Who decides and buys (child request, parent purchase)
   - Post-purchase stage: Usage, sharing, repeat purchase, advocacy
   - Emotional states at each stage
   - Touchpoint preferences (online, retail, social media, influencers)
   - Content consumption patterns (videos, reviews, unboxing, recipes)

4. PAIN POINTS ANALYSIS FOR SNACK CATEGORY
   Identify and prioritize key pain points:
   - For children: Limited snack options they like, peer pressure, health concerns
   - For parents: Nutrition, allergies, portion control, sugar content, cost
   - Taste preferences vs. health tension
   - Availability and convenience issues
   - Cost and value perception (premium pricing for healthy options)
   - Trust in brand claims about nutrition
   - Environmental/sustainability concerns

5. MOTIVATION DRIVERS FOR SNACKING
   Understand what motivates action:
   - Taste and flavor cravings (favorite flavors, fun combinations)
   - Health aspirations (parents: good nutrition; kids: energy, growth)
   - Social recognition (cool snacks, trending brands, shareable)
   - Convenience and on-the-go factor
   - Emotional rewards (celebrate, comfort, celebration)
   - Sustainability and ethical values
   - Gamification and loyalty rewards
